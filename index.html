Research Interest

M.Sc. Project Work at a Glance

During my M.Sc. project at Prof. Prem B. Bisht’s Laboratory, IIT Madras, I had the opportunity to work in a well-established ultrafast optics laboratory, which helped me develop a deep interest in nonlinear light–matter interactions under ultrafast laser excitation.

As part of my project, I learned to synthesize quantum dots from neem leaf extracts for optical limiting applications and subsequently characterized it's properties. This included studying their nonlinear optical response using the Z-scan technique, examining their linear optical behavior through absorption and photoluminescence (PL) measurements, and investigating their physical characteristics.

In addition, I gained hands-on experience with autocorrelation techniques to determine the pulse duration of ultrafast laser pulses and explored dispersion effects arising from pulse propagation through different media. I also studied higher harmonic generation, successfully generating second and third harmonics using nonlinear crystals, and explored multiphoton absorption phenomena in both dye samples and the synthesized quantum dots.

Current Research Interest:

  • Supercontinuum White Light Generation and all-wavelength Z-scan measurements in a single run
  • Quantum Optics and Single Photon Detector
  • Entangled photon generation from spontaneous parametric down conversion (SPDC)
  • Structured Beam (Laguerre Gaussian Vortex type) generation and application
  • Autocorrelation technique using different methods
  • Bragg and Self diffraction experiments
